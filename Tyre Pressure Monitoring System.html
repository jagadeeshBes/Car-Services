Tyre Pressure Monitoring System

Mobile tyres provide expert replacement and repair services for those experiencing issues involving tyre pressure monitoring systems.

TPMS sensors are installed on all modern cars to monitor the tyre pressures on the vehicle and alert you to pressure loss and under inflation.

Properly inflated tyres are essential to the safety of any vehicle and a TPMS sensor warning light will alert you to any issues before a serious incident occurs.

If your TPMS warning light is illuminated it means one of two things. One, there is low pressure in your tyres or two, there has been a system malfunction and there is a problem with your TPMS system.

Either way, you need to act. If you have double checked that your tyres are correctly inflated and the TPMS light remains on, you have a faulty TPMS sensor (or a faulty warning light).

As a primary safety feature, a TPMS light showing on your dashboard will see your vehicle automatically fail its MOT, even if there are no issues with your tyres or tyre valve

What is a Tyre Pressure Monitoring System?

The TPMS on your car or van does exactly what it says on the tin. It is a system that monitors your tyre pressure.

Since 2012, this feature has been mandatory on all passenger vehicles that are produced. A direct TPMS is the most common version of this system and features sensors fitted on the internal part of the valve stems used to inflate your tyres.

More advanced TPMS systems will also monitor other aspects of vehicle health and come with a high temperature warning.

With valves monitoring all four tyres, warning lights and an audio alert will trigger if your tyre is under inflated, or over inflated. The most common cause of a TPMS alert is a puncture to one or more of your tyres.

As mentioned, a TPMS light turned on is an instant MOT failure, so it is vital you act when one occurs.
